Project

Financial Research Paper: Capital Cycles

2024
The project Research Renaissance: Impact of Corporate Actions on Equity Share Prices in the Indian Market explores how major corporate actions — such as dividends, stock splits, buybacks, bonus issues, and demergers — affect stock prices and investor behavior. The core objective of this study is to decode real market reactions and transform them into actionable investment insights. To achieve this, the study integrates both quantitative and behavioral approaches. A combination of real market data (Bloomberg, NSE/BSE, Yahoo Finance) and primary investor insights (147 active participants) was used to build a robust analytical framework. Techniques such as event study methodology, Cumulative Abnormal Returns (CAR), paired t-tests, ANOVA, and regression analysis were applied to evaluate stock price movements across defined time windows. The findings reveal strong and distinct patterns across different corporate actions. Buybacks emerge as the most consistent value-creating signal, delivering ~23% average returns. Demergers stand out as high-risk, high-reward events with extraordinary returns (~143%), driven by market revaluation. Stock splits and bonus issues are largely cosmetic, often causing temporary price distortions rather than real value creation. Dividend announcements show short-term price surges followed by declines, highlighting timing-based opportunities. Beyond numbers, the study emphasizes the role of investor sentiment in shaping market outcomes. Over 60% of investors anticipate price increases before stock splits, while ~58% observe dips after bonus issues, and nearly half report increased trading volumes during corporate actions. Overall, this project delivers a practical decision-making framework for investors, helping them identify which corporate actions create real opportunities and which are driven by market psychology.
